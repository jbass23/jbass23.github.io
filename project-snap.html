An Analysis on an Institution's Social Networks

Project Paper

Source Data

About the Project

In this project, my group was tasked with finding a suitable social network and its corresponding dataset and coming up with a story for what this social network was hoping to accomplish. In our case, we found a dataset that shows an anonymous European research institution as a social network, where each node is an employee and each edge is an instance of an email being sent from one employee to another at a given timestamp (making it a directed temporal graph). Our scenario was that the president of this institution has a hard time organizing social events and came to us to figure out what an efficient way to organize social events would be, while still making sure employees attended.

To accomplish this, we sought to answer a few questions: Which employee(s) in the network have the highest centrality? Are there employees who rarely, if ever, contact colleagues either within or outside their department? And what factors contribute to the formation or dissolution of ties between employees in the network? By answering these questions, we hoped to find which employees spread news throughout the network the fastest, and thus which employees to inform first about social events.

Project Steps

1. Take the directed temporal graph and flatten it to a directed weighted static graph, where the weight is the number of times an employee sends the other employee an email.

2. Calculate the in-degree, out-degree, betweenness, in-closeness, out-closeness, and hub score for all nodes.

3. Find the nodes with the highest ranking in all the previously mentioned metrics and plot the distribution and log-log plots of both in-degree and out-degree. This answers question 1.

4. Plot the graphs of each of the four departments within the whole network. This answers question 2.

5. Take the temporal graph to create a Relational Event Model (REM), which finds causation within a network.

6. Determine if email repetition, email reciprocation, and degree of connectivity have a positive impact on whether or not an email will be sent. This answers question 3.
