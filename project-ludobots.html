What Are Ludobots?

Ludobots are three-dimensional robots that are comprised of non-overlapping links and joints. These links and joints are controlled by a brain, made up of sensors, motors, and a fully connected system of synapses. Each link is is attached to at least on joint that controls its motion, and each joint is a motor that moves depending on the input of sensors (green links) that are connected to them.

These Ludobots are randomly created from scratch; then, their bodies and brains are evolved over the course of tens or hundreds of parallel evolutions in order to find the Ludobot best suited for locomotion in the negative-X direction. This evolution occurs entirely in the background, vastly improving efficiency.

Evolution Steps

1. Ten Ludobots are generated with completely random bodies and brains.

2. Their performance is measured over 1000 'steps' of the simulation.

3. Various synapses and links are randomly mutated from these ten Ludobots, and the evolved Ludobot's performance is measured.

4. If an evolution improves a Ludobot's performance, it is kept. Else, it is scrapped.

5. These ten Ludobots all evolve in parallel over the course of 500 generations.

6. After 500 generations, the best performing Ludobot (of the 5000 different tested Ludobots) is shown.

For 500 generations of ten Ludobots, the full evolution process takes my Macbook only 15-20 minutes.
